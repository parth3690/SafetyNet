## 1. Introduction

Circle ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring transparency about how we collect, use, and share your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains our practices regarding data collection, processing, and your rights under applicable privacy laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for users in the European Economic Area (EEA) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) for California residents.

**Our Privacy Principles:**
- **Transparency**: Clear disclosure of all data collection and use practices
- **User Control**: You control what data is shared and with whom through privacy settings
- **Security**: Industry-standard encryption and security measures protect your data
- **Purpose-Limited**: We use data to provide and improve our services
- **Compliance**: We comply with applicable privacy laws including GDPR and CCPA

## 3. How We Use Your Information

### 3.1 Primary Uses (Service Delivery)

**Safety Monitoring:**
- Learning your daily routines and patterns
- Detecting anomalies and deviations from normal behavior
- Triggering tiered alert system (Tier 1: Check-in, Tier 2: Concern, Tier 3: Emergency)
- Sharing location with circle members during alerts
- Coordinating emergency response

**AI Routine Learning:**
- Processing location data to identify patterns
- Predicting arrival times and detecting delays
- Adapting to schedule changes and exceptions
- Improving alert accuracy and reducing false positives

**Circle Network:**
- Sending alerts to designated circle members
- Facilitating communication between circle members during emergencies
- Managing circle member invitations and permissions

### 3.2 Secondary Uses (Service Enhancement)

**Service Improvement & Personalization:**
- Analyzing usage patterns to improve AI accuracy and reduce false positives
- Identifying safety trends and patterns (aggregated and anonymized)
- Developing new features based on user needs and feedback
- Personalizing your experience (recommended settings, features)
- A/B testing new features and interfaces
- Performance monitoring and optimization

**Analytics & Research:**
- Understanding how users interact with the app
- Measuring feature adoption and effectiveness
- Conducting research on safety patterns (anonymized data)
- Improving algorithms through machine learning
- Generating insights and reports (aggregated data only)

**Communication:**
- Sending safety alerts and check-in notifications
- Providing customer support and responding to inquiries
- Sending important service updates (security, policy changes, new features)
- Marketing communications about Circle and related services (you can opt-out)
- Promotional offers and partnership announcements (with opt-in)

**Advertising & Marketing:**
- Showing you relevant ads within the app (if you opt-in to personalized ads)
- Measuring ad effectiveness and attribution
- Retargeting campaigns (if you visit our website)
- Cross-promotion of Circle features and services
- Partner promotions and offers (with your consent)

### 3.3 Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

For users in the EEA, we process your personal data based on:

1. **Consent** (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR): You provide explicit consent for:
   - Location tracking
   - Audio recording (emergency only)
   - Marketing communications

2. **Contract Performance** (Article 6(1)(b) GDPR): Processing necessary to:
   - Provide safety monitoring services
   - Fulfill our Terms of Service
   - Process emergency alerts

3. **Legitimate Interests** (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR): Processing for:
   - Service improvement, analytics, and AI training
   - Personalization and user experience enhancement
   - Security and fraud prevention
   - Network and information security
   - Marketing and business development (with opt-out rights)
   - Research and development (using anonymized data)

4. **Vital Interests** (Article 6(1)(d) GDPR): Processing to protect:
   - Your life or physical safety during emergencies
   - The safety of your circle members

- We may share aggregated, anonymized data with advertising partners for ad delivery

## 5. Your Rights Under GDPR (EU Users)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have the following rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

### 5.1 Right of Access (Article 15 GDPR)

You have the right to request:
- Confirmation that we process your personal data
- Access to your personal data
- Information about:
  - The purposes of processing
  - Categories of personal data
  - Recipients or categories of recipients
  - Retention periods
  - Your rights to rectification, erasure, restriction, and objection

**How to Exercise**: Submit a data access request through the app (Settings → Privacy → Request Data) or email privacy@circle.app

**Response Time**: Within 30 days (may be extended to 60 days for complex requests)

**Format**: Data provided in machine-readable format (JSON)

### 5.2 Right to Rectification (Article 16 GDPR)

You have the right to:
- Correct inaccurate personal data
- Complete incomplete personal data

**How to Exercise**: Update information directly in the app or contact support

**Response Time**: Immediate (for in-app updates) or within 30 days (for support requests)

### 5.3 Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten") (Article 17 GDPR)

You have the right to request deletion of your personal data when:
- The data is no longer necessary for the original purpose
- You withdraw consent (and no other legal basis exists)
- You object to processing (and no overriding legitimate grounds exist)
- The data has been unlawfully processed
- Deletion is required to comply with legal obligations

**Exceptions**: We may retain data if:
- Required by law (e.g., emergency audio recordings may be retained for legal proceedings)
- Necessary for establishing, exercising, or defending legal claims
- Necessary for public interest (e.g., safety data for research)

**How to Exercise**: 
- Delete account in app (Settings → Account → Delete Account)
- Email privacy@circle.app with subject "GDPR Deletion Request"

**Response Time**: Within 30 days (data purged within 7 days of confirmation)

**Note**: Emergency audio recordings may be retained for up to 1 year for legal/evidentiary purposes, even after account deletion.

### 5.4 Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18 GDPR)

You have the right to restrict processing when:
- You contest the accuracy of data (while we verify)
- Processing is unlawful and you oppose erasure
- We no longer need the data, but you need it for legal claims
- You have objected to processing (while we verify legitimate grounds)

**Effect**: Data will be stored but not processed (except with your consent, for legal claims, or to protect rights)

**How to Exercise**: Contact privacy@circle.app

### 5.5 Right to Data Portability (Article 20 GDPR)

You have the right to:
- Receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format
- Transmit that data to another controller
- Have us transmit data directly to another controller (where technically feasible)

**Applicable Data**: 
- Location history (last 30 days)
- Routine patterns
- Account information
- Circle member list

**Format**: JSON or CSV

**How to Exercise**: Settings → Privacy → Export Data

**Response Time**: Within 30 days

### 5.6 Right to Object (Article 21 GDPR)

You have the right to object to processing based on:
- **Legitimate interests** (Article 6(1)(f)): You can object to processing for service improvement or analytics
- **Direct marketing**: You can opt-out of marketing communications at any time

**How to Exercise**: 
- Marketing: Unsubscribe link in emails or Settings → Notifications
- Legitimate interests: Contact privacy@circle.app

**Response Time**: Immediate (for marketing), within 30 days (for other objections)

### 5.7 Right to Withdraw Consent (Article 7(3) GDPR)

You have the right to withdraw consent at any time for:
- Location tracking
- Audio recording
- Marketing communications
- Social media integration

**Effect**: Withdrawal does not affect lawfulness of processing before withdrawal

**How to Exercise**: Update preferences in app settings or contact support

### 5.8 Right to Lodge a Complaint (Article 77 GDPR)

You have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority (DPA) if you believe we have violated GDPR.

**EU DPAs**: List available at https://edpb.europa.eu/about-edpb/board/members_en

**UK**: Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) - https://ico.org.uk

**How to File**: Contact your local DPA directly

## 6. Your Rights Under CCPA (California Users)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

### 6.1 Right to Know (CCPA § 1798.100)

You have the right to request:
- **Categories of personal information** we collect
- **Categories of sources** from which we collect information
- **Business or commercial purposes** for collecting or selling information
- **Categories of third parties** with whom we share information
- **Specific pieces of personal information** we have collected about you

**Categories We Collect** (as defined by CCPA):
- **Identifiers**: Phone number, email address, name
- **Geolocation Data**: GPS coordinates, location history
- **Audio/Electronic Data**: Emergency audio recordings
- **Internet Activity**: App usage, interaction data
- **Inferences**: Routine patterns, predicted behavior

**How to Exercise**: 
- Submit request through app (Settings → Privacy → Request Data)
- Email privacy@circle.app with subject "CCPA Data Request"
- Call toll-free: 1-800-CIRCLE-1 (if available)

**Response Time**: Within 45 days (may be extended to 90 days with notice)

**Verification**: We may require identity verification to prevent fraud

**Format**: Data provided in portable format (JSON, CSV)

### 6.2 Right to Delete (CCPA § 1798.105)

You have the right to request deletion of personal information we have collected, subject to exceptions:

**Exceptions** (we may retain data if):
- Necessary to complete a transaction or provide requested service
- Required by law or legal obligation
- Used for internal purposes compatible with context of collection
- Necessary for security, debugging, or functionality
- Necessary for research (if anonymized)

**How to Exercise**:
- Delete account in app (Settings → Account → Delete Account)
- Email privacy@circle.app with subject "CCPA Deletion Request"

**Response Time**: Within 45 days (data purged within 7 days of confirmation)

**Verification**: Identity verification required

### 6.3 Right to Opt-Out of Sale (CCPA § 1798.120)

**Data Sharing for Advertising:**
Under CCPA, sharing personal information (including advertising identifiers and usage data) with advertising partners may constitute a "sale" even if no money changes hands. We share data with advertising partners for personalized advertising (with your consent).

**How to Opt-Out:**
- **In-App**: Settings → Privacy → Do Not Sell My Personal Information
- **Email**: privacy@circle.app with subject "CCPA Opt-Out"
- **Website**: Visit our website and click "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" link
- **Toll-Free**: 1-800-CIRCLE-1 (if available)

**Response Time**: We honor opt-out requests within 15 business days

**Effect of Opt-Out:**
- You will still see ads, but they will be less personalized
- Some features that rely on personalized data may be limited
- We will not share your personal information with advertising partners
- We will continue to use your data for core safety services

### 6.4 Right to Opt-Out of Sharing (CPRA)

You have the right to opt-out of "sharing" personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

**Our Practice**: We do not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

**If we change this practice**, we will provide opt-out mechanisms.

### 6.5 Right to Non-Discrimination (CCPA § 1798.125)

We will not discriminate against you for exercising your CCPA rights. We will not:
- Deny goods or services
- Charge different prices
- Provide different quality of service
- Suggest you will receive different treatment

**Note**: Some features may require certain data (e.g., location tracking for safety monitoring). If you opt-out of essential data collection, some features may be unavailable.

### 6.6 Right to Correct (CPRA)

You have the right to request correction of inaccurate personal information.

**How to Exercise**: Update information in app or contact privacy@circle.app

**Response Time**: Within 45 days

### 6.7 Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information (CPRA)

You have the right to limit use of sensitive personal information to:
- Providing requested services
- Security and fraud prevention
- Short-term transient use
- Performing services on behalf of the business
- Ensuring safety of natural persons

**Sensitive Information We Collect**:
- Precise geolocation
- Audio recordings (emergency only)

**How to Exercise**: Settings → Privacy → Limit Sensitive Data Use

**Note**: Limiting sensitive data use may impact safety monitoring functionality.

### 6.8 Authorized Agent Requests

You may designate an authorized agent to submit CCPA requests on your behalf. The agent must:
- Provide written authorization
- Verify their own identity
- Provide proof of your identity

**How to Submit**: Email privacy@circle.app with authorization documentation

### 6.9 Disclosure of Financial Incentives (CCPA § 1798.125)

**Current Practice**: We do not offer financial incentives for data collection.

**If we offer incentives in the future**, we will:
- Provide clear notice of material terms
- Obtain opt-in consent
- Allow opt-out at any time
- Provide good-faith estimate of value of data

## 7. Data Security

### 7.1 Security Measures

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your personal information:

**Encryption:**
- **In Transit**: TLS 1.3 encryption for all data transmission
- **At Rest**: AES-256 encryption for stored data
- **End-to-End**: Location data encrypted before transmission

**Access Controls:**
- Multi-factor authentication for administrative access
- Role-based access controls (RBAC)
- Regular access audits and reviews
- Employee training on data protection

**Network Security:**
- Firewalls and intrusion detection systems
- Regular security assessments and penetration testing
- DDoS protection
- Secure API endpoints

**Application Security:**
- Code reviews and security audits
- Bug bounty program ($500-$5,000 per vulnerability)
- Regular dependency updates
- Secure coding practices

**Physical Security:**
- Data centers with 24/7 monitoring
- Biometric access controls
- Environmental controls (fire suppression, backup power)

### 7.2 Incident Response

In the event of a data breach:
- **Detection**: Automated monitoring and alerting
- **Assessment**: Immediate evaluation of scope and impact
- **Notification**: 
  - Affected users notified within 72 hours (GDPR requirement)
  - Regulatory authorities notified within 72 hours (if required)
  - Public disclosure if breach poses significant risk
- **Remediation**: Immediate steps to contain and fix the breach
- **Post-Incident Review**: Analysis and improvement of security measures

### 7.3 Your Role in Security

You play an important role in protecting your data:
- Use strong, unique passwords
- Enable biometric authentication (Face ID, Touch ID)
- Keep your app updated
- Don't share your account credentials
- Be cautious of phishing attempts
- Review your circle members regularly
- Report suspicious activity immediately

## 8. Data Retention

### 8.1 Retention Periods

**Location Data:**
- **Active Monitoring**: Retained for 30 days (rolling window) for active users
- **Emergency Events**: Retained for 1 year (for legal/evidentiary purposes)
- **Routine Patterns**: Stored on device, synced to server for 90 days
- **Historical Location**: Aggregated location data may be retained longer for service improvement (anonymized)
- **Inactive Users**: Location data deleted after 90 days of inactivity

**Account Information:**
- **Active Accounts**: Retained while account is active
- **Deleted Accounts**: Purged within 7 days of deletion request
- **Inactive Accounts**: Deleted after 2 years of inactivity

**Audio Recordings:**
- **Emergency Recordings**: Retained for 1 year
- **Deleted Upon Request**: Within 30 days (subject to legal holds)

**Circle Member Data:**
- **Active Circles**: Retained while user is active
- **Removed Members**: Contact information deleted within 30 days

**Analytics & Logs:**
- **Usage Analytics**: Aggregated, anonymized data retained for 3 years (for service improvement and research)
- **Error Logs**: Retained for 90 days
- **Access Logs**: Retained for 1 year (for security auditing)
- **Advertising Data**: Retained per advertising partner policies (typically 1-2 years)
- **Marketing Data**: Retained while you are an active user, deleted upon account deletion

### 8.2 Deletion Process

When you request deletion:
1. **Immediate**: Account marked for deletion, services suspended
2. **Within 7 Days**: 
   - Personal data purged from active databases
   - Location history deleted
   - Circle member data removed
3. **Within 30 Days**:
   - Data removed from backup systems
   - Audio recordings deleted (unless legal hold)
4. **Within 90 Days**:
   - Anonymized data removed from analytics
   - All traces removed from systems

**Legal Holds**: If data is subject to legal proceedings, we may retain it beyond normal retention periods until the matter is resolved.

## Appendix C: Glossary

**Personal Information**: Information that identifies, relates to, or could reasonably be linked with you.

**Processing**: Any operation performed on personal data (collection, storage, use, deletion, etc.).

**Controller**: The entity that determines the purposes and means of processing (Circle).

**Processor**: An entity that processes data on behalf of the controller (e.g., cloud providers).

**Consent**: Freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous agreement to processing.

**Legitimate Interests**: Processing necessary for our legitimate business interests (balanced against your rights).

**Vital Interests**: Processing necessary to protect life or physical safety.

**Data Subject**: The individual whose personal data is processed (you).

**Data Protection Authority (DPA)**: Independent public authority that supervises GDPR compliance.
